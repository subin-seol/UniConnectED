# How to calculate story points

Story points aren't easily measured [[link](https://www.reddit.com/r/agile/comments/g9nw8q/how_do_you_actually_estimate_with_story_points/)]. It is not a direct measure of hours but rather a relative measure of effort and complexity on the task. For now, our approach is to use Fibonacci sequence for the story point estimation. The number does not directly calulate the amount of the work of the user story, but reflects the relative level of uncertainty and effort needs to make to accomplish the tasks compared to others. The higher the number, the more complex the task is.  

One of the benefitial of using Fibonacci sequence number is that, the estimation is done through relative comparison, every task can be estimated against the previous one to ensure interpretability and consistency

Here are the definitions of how the story points define our user story:
- 1–2 points: Very small, straightforward tasks (low effort, low risk).
- 3–5 points: Medium tasks with moderate effort or dependencies.
- 8–13 points: Large or complex tasks requiring significant time, coordination, or technical learning.

The story points estimated for [[ User stories ]] were decided by group consensus over Zoom, where each team member first proposed a number, then had a chance to defend their estimation before reaching a final agreement. Meeting minutes can be found [here.](Meetings#extra-group-meeting-2)
