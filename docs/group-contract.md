**Group Contract – Software Project (COMP90082)**

**Group Name:** UC-Koala

This group contract serves as a tangible representation of our group’s mutual commitments for the duration of the project.

**1. The Project Journey**

**Destination (Goals)**

1. Achieve a minimum grade of 80% for the project.
2. Learn about the web development landscape.
3. Become familiar with Agile methodologies.

**Route (Values)**

1. Ownership - Clear distinction of work means teams can work effectively.
2. Customer Obsession - Leaders start with the customer and work backwards. They work vigorously to earn and keep customer trust. Although leaders pay attention to competitors, they obsess over customers.
3. Learn and Be Curious - Leaders are never done learning and always seek to improve themselves. They are curious about new possibilities and act to explore them.

**Obstacles (Concerns)**

1. Unequal participation - Some members may contribute more than others, leading to imbalances in workload and input.
2. Lack of ownership - Some members may not take full responsibility for their tasks, leading to gaps in progress and accountability.

**2. Group Commitments**

**Communication**

| **Potential Issue**                                           | **Response**                                                                                                                                          |
| ------------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Group members disagree about a course of action               | Hold a team discussion to understand all perspectives. Use majority vote if consensus cannot be reached. Escalate to mentor if disagreement persists. |
| Group member(s) are being rude or dismissive to other members | Address the behavior immediately in a private conversation. If behavior continues, bring to team discussion and potentially involve mentor.           |

We will use **Slack for daily communication and GitHub Projects for task management and progress tracking**.

We expect all group members to **show respect to others, listen actively to different opinions, provide constructive feedback, and respond to messages within 24 hours**.

If there are repeated issues among group members, our course of action will be to **first attempt resolution through team discussion, then escalate to our mentor, and finally contact lecturers if necessary**.

**Responsibilities and Workload**

| **Potential Issue**                          | **Response**                                                                                                                                             |
| -------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Group member(s) cannot meet a deadline       | Notify the team as soon as possible via Slack. Team will redistribute tasks or provide assistance. If recurring, escalate to mentor for guidance.        |
| Group member(s) are overburdened by workload | Immediately communicate workload concerns to the team. Redistribute tasks fairly and consider breaking down large tasks into smaller, manageable pieces. |

**Attendance and Participation**

| **Potential Issue**                            | **Response**                                                                                                                                                       |
| ---------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Group member(s) do not show up to meetings     | Member must notify team 24 hours in advance when possible. We will provide meeting recordings and detailed minutes. Repeated absences will be escalated to mentor. |
| Group member(s) are not participating in Slack | Reach out to member individually to check if there are any issues. If non-participation continues, discuss as a team and potentially involve mentor.               |

The consequences of repeated absences will be **first a team discussion to understand underlying issues, then escalation to mentor, and finally documentation of participation levels for assessment purposes**.
