## Project Timeline

Below is the overall semester timeline for our software project. The plan is divided into four main sprints, each with clear deliverables and checkpoints. The timeline also includes buffer time, presentations, and final handover.  

<img width="1158" height="600" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/241c2cd6-eb54-4830-980e-75d21b9763de" />


### Timeline Breakdown
- **Sprint 1 (Weeks 1–5)**  
  - Requirement gathering, design, and initial setup.  
  - Early development focus on laying the foundation for core functionality.  

- **Sprint 2 (Weeks 6–8)**  
  - 45% development in Week 6 followed by 35% in Weeks 7–8.  
  - Prioritises feature development and integration of core modules (i.e., public employment opportunities)

- **Buffer (Week 9)**  
  - Dedicated time for addressing unexpected blockers, delays, or technical challenges.  

- **Sprint 3 (Weeks 10–12)**  
  - Final 20% of development and any extension work.  
  - Final presentation and demo of the working product.  

<img width="1158" height="628" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/f18edabc-b411-4c0d-8611-2f4123128fd3" />


- **Sprint 4 (Weeks 13–14)**  
  - Handover and documentation phase.  
  - Ensures long-term maintainability and knowledge transfer.  

<img width="1158" height="600" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/241c2cd6-eb54-4830-980e-75d21b9763de" />

### Key Notes
- The timeline accounts for both development progress and milestones.  
- A buffer period is included to absorb risks and maintain delivery quality.  
- Final presentation and handover phases are crucial for demonstrating outcomes and ensuring sustainability.
